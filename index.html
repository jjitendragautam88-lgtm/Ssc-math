Basics of Percentage
Foundation — Yahan se shuru karo
CGLNTPCCBT-2
💡 BASIC MEANING
Percent ka matlab hai — "per 100" yaani "100 mein se kitna".

Agar class mein 100 bacche hain aur 40 pass hue → pass% = 40%
Agar 60 bacche hain aur 24 pass hue → 24/60 × 100 = 40%

Simple formula: Percentage = (Part ÷ Whole) × 100
📐 FORMULAS
x% of y =x × y / 100
Part → % =(Part ÷ Whole) × 100
% → Value =% × Total / 100
A% of B =B% of A ✓ (swap trick!)
⚡ SHORT TRICKS
✦15% of 60 = 60% of 15 = 9 (swap trick — jo easy lage woh karo!)
✦x% increase → number × (100+x)/100
✦x% decrease → number × (100−x)/100
✦10% of any number = wo number ÷ 10 (aur 5% = uska aadha)
✦1% = number ÷ 100, baaki sab isse nikaalte hain
⚠️ COMMON MISTAKE
⚠️ "A is 20% more than B" aur "B is 20% less than A" — ye DONO SAME NAHI hain! Base change hoti hai — bahut log ye galti karte hain!
📝 SOLVED EXAMPLE
Q: Ek class mein 80 students hain. 60% pass hue. Kitne fail hue?
Step 1: Pass = 60% of 80 = 60×80/100 = 48
Step 2: Fail = 80 − 48 = 32
✅ Answer: 32 students fail hue
🏋️ PRACTICE QUESTIONS
Q1
350 ka 14% kitna hoga?
✅ 14×350/100 = 49
Q2
Box mein 200 toffees hain. Ramesh ne 35% kha liye. Kitni bachin?
✅ 35% of 200 = 70. Baaki = 200−70 = 130
Q3
56 is what percent of 280?
✅ (56/280) × 100 = 20%

Type 1: Successive % Change
Net change formula — CGL mein har saal aata hai!
CGL MainsCBT-2
💡 MEANING
Jab kisi number par pehle a% change ho, phir b% change ho — dono baar ka base ALAG hota hai. Isliye simple add/subtract nahi kar sakte!

Example: Price 20% badhi, phir 10% giri. Net = 20−10+(20×−10)/100 = 10−2 = 8%
📐 FORMULA
Net % Change =a + b + (a×b)/100
Increase ho toh:a, b = positive (+)
Decrease ho toh:a, b = negative (−)
Mixed ho toh:e.g. a=+20, b=−10
💡 Net % = a + b + ab/100 — ye formula ratta maar lo! CGL mein har saal aata hai.
⚡ SHORT TRICKS
✦Same % increase then decrease: Net = −(a²/100)% ALWAYS
✦Example: 20% up then 20% down = −(400/100) = −4% (NOT zero!)
✦ab/100 — dono same sign: positive; different sign: negative
✦10% up then 10% down = −(100/100) = −1%
⚠️ COMMON MISTAKE
⚠️ Log sochte hain 20% up + 20% down = 0%. GALAT! Net = 20+(−20)+(20×−20)/100 = 0−4 = −4%. Isliye formula ZAROORI hai!
📝 SOLVED EXAMPLE
Q: Phone ki price pehle 10% badhi, phir 20% badhi. Net % change?
a = +10, b = +20
Formula: a + b + ab/100 = 10 + 20 + (10×20)/100
Calculate: 30 + 200/100 = 30 + 2 = 32%
✅ Net increase = 32%
🏋️ PRACTICE QUESTIONS
Q1
Vastu ki price 15% badhi, phir 15% giri. Net % change?
✅ a=+15, b=−15. Net = 15−15+(15×−15)/100 = 0−225/100 = −2.25% decrease
Q2
Population pehle 20% badhi, phir 25% badhi. Overall % change?
✅ a=+20, b=+25. Net = 20+25+(20×25)/100 = 45+5 = 50% increase
Q3
Price 30% increase hua, phir kitna % decrease kare ki original price mile?
✅ Multiplier method: 1.3 × x = 1 → x = 1/1.3. Decrease = (1−1/1.3)/1 × 100 = 30/130×100 = 23.07%

Type 2: Series Concept (Multiplier Method)
Chain se percentage changes — fastest method!
💡 MEANING
Jab multiple % changes ek ke baad ek lagate hain, har baar nayi value base banti hai. Iska best tarika hai Multiplier Method.

x% increase → Multiply by (100+x)/100
x% decrease → Multiply by (100−x)/100
📐 FORMULA
Final Value =Initial × M1 × M2 × M3...
+20% multiplier =× 1.2
−15% multiplier =× 0.85
+10%, −20% =× 1.1 × 0.8 = × 0.88 = 12% decrease
⚡ MULTIPLIER QUICK LIST
✦+10%→1.1 | +20%→1.2 | +25%→1.25 | +50%→1.5 | +100%→2
✦−10%→0.9 | −20%→0.8 | −25%→0.75 | −50%→0.5
✦Final multiplier < 1 → overall decrease; > 1 → overall increase
⚠️ COMMON MISTAKE
⚠️ 10% + 20% = 30% NAHI! Multipliers multiply hote hain: 1.1 × 1.2 = 1.32 → 32% increase (30% nahi!)
📝 SOLVED EXAMPLE
Q: Rs. 5000 ki salary: pehle 20% increase, phir 10% decrease. Final salary?
Multipliers: 1.2 × 0.9 = 1.08
Final: 5000 × 1.08 = Rs. 5400
✅ Net 8% increase → Rs. 5400
🏋️ PRACTICE QUESTIONS
Q1
Rs. 8000 pe 25% increase, phir 20% decrease. Final value?
✅ 8000 × 1.25 × 0.8 = 8000 × 1.0 = Rs. 8000 (Net 0% change!)
Q2
Population 10% badhti hai 3 saal tak. Initial 1000. Final?
✅ 1000 × 1.1³ = 1000 × 1.331 = 1331
Q3
Price mein 3 baar 20% decrease. Initial Rs. 10000. Final?
✅ 10000 × 0.8³ = 10000 × 0.512 = Rs. 5120

💡 MEANING

📐 FORMULA

✅ Net = 65% increase

✅ 13310 / 1.1³ = 13310 / 1.331 = Rs. 10,000

Type 4: AB Type — Comparison Questions
"A is x% more than B" → "B is ?% less than A"
CGL Most Repeated
💡 MEANING
"A is 20% more than B" → A = 120, B = 100 (B hai base)
"B is how much % less than A?" → ab A base hai (120). B = 100 → 20/120 × 100 = 16.67% less

Base change hoti hai — isliye answer same nahi hota!
📐 GOLDEN FORMULAS
A x% MORE than B →B is x/(100+x)×100 % LESS than A
A x% LESS than B →B is x/(100−x)×100 % MORE than A
⚡ COMMON PAIRS — RATTA MAAR LO
✦20% more ↔ 16.67% less (= 1/6)
✦25% more ↔ 20% less (= 1/5)
✦33.33% more ↔ 25% less (= 1/4)
✦50% more ↔ 33.33% less (= 1/3)
✦100% more ↔ 50% less
✦10% less ↔ 11.11% more | 20% less ↔ 25% more
⚠️ COMMON MISTAKE
⚠️ "A is 25% more than B" → sochte hain "B is 25% less than A". GALAT! B is only 20% less. Formula use karo, andaza nahi!
📝 SOLVED EXAMPLE
Q: Ram ki salary Shyam se 40% zyada. Shyam ki salary Ram se kitne % kam?
Formula: B less than A = 40/(100+40) × 100
Calculate: 40/140 × 100 = 2/7 × 100 = 28.57%
✅ Shyam is 28.57% (= 2/7) less than Ram
🏋️ PRACTICE QUESTIONS
Q1
A is 50% more than B. B is how much % less than A?
✅ 50/(150) × 100 = 33.33%
Q2
X's income 20% less than Y's. Y is how much % more than X?
✅ 20/(100−20) × 100 = 20/80 × 100 = 25%
Q3
A is 2/5 more than B. B is how much % less than A?
✅ 2/5 more = 40% more. B less than A = 40/140×100 = 28.57%

Type 6: Weighted Average %
Different groups ka overall % nikalna
💡 MEANING
Jab 2 ya zyada groups hain, aur har group ka % alag ho, toh overall % simple average se alag hoti hai. Weight (size) matter karta hai!
📐 FORMULA
Overall % =(n1×p1 + n2×p2) / (n1+n2)
Where:n = group size, p = percentage
📝 SOLVED EXAMPLE
Q: Factory A: 200 workers, 60% male. Factory B: 300 workers, 40% male. Overall male%?
Males in A: 200 × 0.6 = 120
Males in B: 300 × 0.4 = 120
Total: 240/500 × 100 = 48% (simple avg 50% nahi!)
✅ Overall Male% = 48%
🏋️ PRACTICE QUESTIONS
Q1
Test 1: 100 marks, 70% score. Test 2: 150 marks, 80% score. Overall %?
✅ Marks: 70+120=190. Total: 250. Overall = 190/250×100 = 76%
Q2
Village A: 500 pop, 30% literate. Village B: 1000 pop, 45% literate. Combined literacy%?
✅ (500×30 + 1000×45) / 1500 = (15000+45000)/1500 = 60000/1500 = 40%
Q3
Class A: 40 students, 75% passed. Class B: 60 students, 65% passed. Overall pass%?
✅ (40×75 + 60×65)/100 = (3000+3900)/100 = 69%

Votes, margin, winner% — all election calculations
CGLCBT-2
💡 MEANING

Margin =(W% − L%) of Total votes

Type 9: Income, Expenditure & Saving
Salary + expenditure change → savings ka % change
💡 GOLDEN RULE
Saving = Income − Expenditure

Saving par koi direct shortcut nahi — dono alag calculate karo, phir saving nikalo. Numbers assume karo (100 ya given value).
📐 STEPS
Step 1:Old Saving = Income − Expenditure
Step 2:New Income = Old × (1+income%/100)
Step 3:New Exp = Old × (1+exp%/100)
Step 4:New Saving = New Income − New Exp
Step 5:(New−Old)/Old × 100 = Saving change%
📝 SOLVED EXAMPLE
Q: Income Rs. 10000, Exp Rs. 8000. Income 20% badha, Exp 10% badha. Saving % change?
Old saving: 10000−8000 = 2000
New Income: 10000×1.2 = 12000
New Exp: 8000×1.1 = 8800
New Saving: 12000−8800 = 3200
Change%: (3200−2000)/2000×100 = 60% increase
✅ Savings 60% increase hue
🏋️ PRACTICE QUESTIONS
Q1
Income Rs. 5000, spending Rs. 4000. Income 10% badha, spending 15% badha. Savings %change?
✅ Old saving=1000. New I=5500, New E=4600. New S=900. Change = (900-1000)/1000×100 = −10% decrease
Q2
Income ka 80% kharcha. Income 20% badha, expenditure 25% badha. Savings% change?
✅ Old: I=100, E=80, S=20. New: I=120, E=100, S=20. Savings same (0% change)!
Q3
Income Rs. 15000, saves 1/3 of income. Income 20% badhi, expenditure 10% badhi. New savings?
✅ Old: E=10000, S=5000. New I=18000, New E=11000. New S = Rs. 7000

Only A =n(A) − n(A∩B)
Only B =n(B) − n(A∩B)

✅ Only Maths = 30%, Neither = 20%
